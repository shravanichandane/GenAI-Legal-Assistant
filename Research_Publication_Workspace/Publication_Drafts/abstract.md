# Abstract

This paper presents a comparative evaluation of dense retrieval architectures for legal contract intelligence. The problem of efficiently and accurately retrieving relevant clauses from legal documents is critical for automating contract review. We evaluate two prominent embedding models, all-MiniLM-L6-v2 and nlpaueb/legal-bert-base-uncased, using the CUAD and ContractNLI datasets. Our methodology contrasts dense retrieval using FAISS indexing with traditional sparse retrieval (BM25). The results demonstrate that domain-specific models like Legal-BERT provide superior ranking quality and semantic understanding of legal nuances, while MiniLM offers significant advantages in latency and index size. Furthermore, FAISS-based dense retrieval outperforms BM25 in capturing semantic relationships. We conclude by discussing the trade-offs between effectiveness and efficiency, and outline future work involving Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and domain-specific fine-tuning.
